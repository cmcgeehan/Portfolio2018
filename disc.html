How It Started

As a sophomore studying computer science and design, I always felt that while I could see the way my work in design class could truly help people and create change, the programming assignments given were abstract and it was hard to invision how they could translate into real world impact. Develop + Innovate for Social Change was my solution to this problem: a student group where programmers, designers and other problem solvers could come together to help social impact groups solve the technological challenges they could not solve on their own.

I'm very excited for the work we are doing this year, working with the Chicago Furniture Bank and the Evanston Development Cooperative.
