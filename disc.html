How it Began

The computer science students I knew at Northwestern often seemed disappointed that the work they did for school. I realized that nonprofits often could not afford programmers to help them solve their pressing issues. By connecting these two groups, Northwestern students could be fulfilled by the work they do, while creating real change in our community.

To date, 35 students have worked on five projects for nonprofits and coops ranging from route optimization for furniture banks to zoning visualizations for affordable housing projects. We plan to offer our projects as a for-credit studio through Northwestern's computer science department next year.

I'm very excited for the work we are doing this year, working with the Chicago Furniture Bank and the Evanston Development Cooperative.
