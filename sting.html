Sting

Industrial Design - Northwestern University

Problem

This assignment was the for which we were given the widest range of possibilities - any shoe design was allowed. I chose to continue with the thread of work for the Evanston boxing club, and design a new boxing shoe. I had never worn boxing shoes and wanted to learn more about them.

Pain Points

An important piece of boxing shoes is the grip on the bottom. Boxers are constantly making sharp movements, and need shoes that will provide traction when they quickly get out of the way of an opponent's punch.

Another important consideration for boxing shoes is to make them as light as possible, so that boxers can be nimble.

Thumbnail drawings of some initial concepts.

Solution

My final design sport Under Armour's logo, as does all Northwestern athletic gear, and has straps that fit in with Under Armour's current style.

  • Lightweight The top of these shoes are made out of a tight mesh, allowing them to slip on like a sock. This takes away the need for shoelaces, and makes them even lighter.
